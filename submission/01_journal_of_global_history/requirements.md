> **Publisher:** Cambridge University Press
> **ISSN:** 1740-0228 (print) / 1740-0236 (online)
> **Portal:** https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-global-history
> **Submit via:** Cambridge online portal (separate account from Cambridge Core)

---

## Why This Journal?

- Tvedt (2010) — the paper we directly build on — was published here
- Interdisciplinary readership receptive to NLP + econometrics methodology
- Strong Great Divergence tradition (directly engages Pomeranz, Broadberry, Tvedt)
- Less heavy econometric scrutiny than pure cliometrics journals → our collapsed DiD power limitation is less of a liability
- Canal illustrations (England vs. Asia) directly serve the journal's comparative global framing

## Formatting Requirements

### Manuscript
- [ ] **Word count:** 8,000–12,500 words including footnotes
- [ ] **Format:** Flexible format accepted at initial submission (no strict template)
- [ ] **Full footnotes required** even at initial submission
- [ ] **File type:** Word (.docx) or LaTeX

### Front Matter
- [ ] **Abstract:** 100–150 words
- [ ] **Keywords:** 5–10 keywords
- [ ] **Title page:** Separate file containing:
  - Author name(s)
  - Affiliation(s) (institution, department, city, country)
  - Corresponding author with active email
  - ORCID (if available)
  - Acknowledgments
  - Funding statement
  - Competing interests declaration

### Peer Review
- **Double-anonymous** — manuscript must be completely anonymized
- Remove all author-identifying information from the manuscript body
- Author details go ONLY on the separate title page

### References
- Footnote/endnote style (not author-date) — verify against recent JGH articles
- Check recent issues for exact citation conventions

### Figures & Tables
- Figures submitted electronically
- High resolution (300 dpi minimum for photographs, 1200 dpi for line art)
- Captions included in the manuscript text, not in figure files

### Data & Replication
- No mandatory replication package (unlike EEH)
- Data Availability Statement recommended but not strictly required
- GitHub replication repo is a strong voluntary addition
