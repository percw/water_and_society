# The Intelligence Commodification Thesis
### "Are We at 1760?" — Quantifying the Linguistic Shift from Human Wisdom to Machine Commodity

## Hypothesis

The linguistic commodification of *intelligence* — from "wisdom", "genius", "soul", "imagination" (sacred/human) to "algorithm", "software", "data processing", "machine learning" (commodity/machine) — follows the identical trajectory as water's 18th-century commodification. The crossover has already passed (~2012-2016). This predicts a GDP divergence between AI-adopting and non-adopting economies within 20-30 years.

## Data Sources

1. **Google Books Ngram Corpus (English, 1950-2019)** — frequency trajectories for intelligence vocabulary
2. **Maddison Project Database 2023 / World Bank** — GDP per capita for treatment and control economies
3. **OECD AI Policy Observatory** — supplementary data on national AI adoption timing

## Vocabulary Taxonomy

### Sacred/Human Intelligence (expected: declining or flat)
- wisdom, genius, intellect, consciousness, intuition, imagination, soul, reasoning, judgment, discernment

### Commodity/Machine Intelligence (expected: rising, especially post-2010)
- algorithm, software, database, optimization, artificial intelligence, computer program, data processing, machine learning, neural network, automation

## Treatment / Control Design

- **Treatment:** USA (AI leader since 1956 Dartmouth Conference)
- **European Controls:** Germany, France, Italy (similar development, different AI intensity)
- **Asian Comparisons:** South Korea, Japan (tech-heavy but different timing)
- **Developing Controls:** Brazil, India, Nigeria
